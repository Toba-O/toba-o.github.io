    Use public transport

    The greenhouse gases emitted from cars and other personal transport devices tend to emit quite a bit of carbon dioxide per capita. Transport such as trains and busses can carry more people while producing less carbon dioxide per capita. Switching can also lower your individual transportation costs.

    Fly Direct

    Flying has the largest negative impact on the global carbon footprint. While flying, attempt to take the least number of stops possible so that the total flying distance is conserved.

    Offset your carbon while traveling

    Some transportation companies have developed programs that enable users to pay additional money on top of their ticket that go to funding initiatives to reduce the carbon footprint. Do consider paying this additional money as it greatly assists the environment.

    Pack a water bottle

    Having a reusable water bottle can help you limit plastic consumption while traveling. This can in turn cut the energy consumed in production and transportation needed to produce plastic bottles.

    Switch to Electric Vehicles

    Electric vehicles do wonders for the environment when compared to gasoline powered cars. Also, with the rising gas prices, electric vehicles can help avoid headaches in the future.
