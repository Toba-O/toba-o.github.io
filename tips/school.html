    Raise awarness

    Your school might not understand issues surrounding the carbon footprint and climate change. This can be changed with in-school initiatives (school projects and fundraising events) that can help educate the students and staff.

    Compost

    With all the food waste that is produced in school, setting up a composting center in your school can lead to many benefits for the environment by lower the methane emissions that are being produced.

    Switching off computers

    The computers in your school eat up a lot of energy and when they are not in use, make sure to turn them off so that the school can keep its carbon footprint low.

    Use local food

    If you can eat local food, do it. This helps the environment in so many ways as energy can be saved by not needing to transport goods from out-stationed regions and instead eating local grown food.

    Take local trips

    Traveling to far-flung destations take a toll on the environment as transport requires a large amount of energy. That energy can be saved by visiting nearby destinations and will in turn help the environment.
