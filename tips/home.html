    Insulate your Home

    Heating bills are expensive and consume a lot of energy. Insulating allows for heat to be retained in your home and for less energy to be used.

    Switch To Renewables

    Moving to solar, wind, or hydroelectric energy can allow for reduction of household emissions and money being saved on energy bills in the long run.

    Buy Energy Efficient

    Energy efficient appliances are all around us. From eco-friendly lightbulbs to energy saving dishwashers, there are many ways to help reduce the world's carbon footprint.

    Use Less Water

    The process of bringing water to our homes requires tons of energy (especially to heat it). By using less water (i.e when bathing, brushing teeth, etc.) you will benefit yourself and the environment.

    Consider your Diet

    Meat products require a lot of land, water, and energy to produce (for feeding, housing, and providing water for the animal). These products also promote the creation of methane (a greenhouse gas harmful to the environment). By attempting to eat less meat, specifically red meat, you help reduce the global carbon footprint.
